National Trust Connect: The "Super-App" Ecosystem

A unified "Phys-gital" platform combining location-based AR with global streaming

Solution Overview

A unified "Phys-gital" platform that combines a location-based AR companion for on-site visitors with a global streaming service for remote access. This "Super App" allows users to gamify their physical visits (Pokemon-Go style) while also offering a "Netflix-style" library of 360-degree tours, nature streams, and wellbeing classes for home use.

Key Features
  • On-Site: AR "Wildlife Quest" with GPS challenges, virtual artifact collection, and digital rewards (e.g., free coffee)
  • Off-Site: "National Trust Stream" featuring live slow-TV nature feeds, expert history documentaries, and virtual wellbeing sessions
  • Unified Profile: Points earned physically (walking trails) unlock digital content (exclusive documentaries) and vice-versa
Benefits

Solves the "Dual Crisis" of relevance and access simultaneously. Increases dwell time and youth engagement on-site through gamification, while monetising the global "armchair" audience who cannot visit physically. Creates a seamless loop where digital content drives physical visits and physical visits unlock digital rewards.

Strategic Challenge
Declining youth engagement & geographical limitations
New to Organization?
Yes
Key Data & Insight

Research shows a "hybrid paradox": Gen Z demands "active, gamified" physical experiences (Euromonitor) but also consumes high volumes of "digital wellness" content at home. Merging these creates a 360-degree value proposition that standalone apps cannot match.

Drawbacks

High complexity in app development (requires both high-fidelity video streaming and real-time geolocation AR). Risk of app "bloat" if features are not intuitive.

National Trust "Flexi-Go" (Pay-As-You-Go Smart Pass)

Digital smart wallet replacing rigid annual membership with flexible pay-per-visit

Solution Overview

A digital "smart wallet" that replaces the rigid annual membership with a flexible, pay-as-you-go model. Allows younger, budget-conscious users to pay for entry via mobile app on a per-visit basis, featuring dynamic pricing (discounts for off-peak/rainy days) and loyalty accumulation.

Key Features
  • "Scan-and-Go" mobile QR entry (Oyster-card style)
  • Dynamic pricing engine (lower prices on weekdays)
  • Digital loyalty stamp card (e.g., "5th visit free")
  • Integrated parking and café payments
Benefits

Removes the high upfront cost barrier (£80+) for students and young adults. Captures the "spontaneous" visitor market who prefer last-minute bookings and monetises casual visitors who refuse to commit to a full annual contract.

Strategic Challenge
Rigid subscription model vs. commitment-phobic Gen Z
New to Organization?
Yes
Key Data & Insight

Customers—especially Gen Z—are increasingly opting for "pay-on-the-day" flexibility over annual commitments due to economic pressures. Additionally, 46% of bookings are now mobile, validating the need for a seamless digital entry system.

Drawbacks

Risk of "cannibalisation" (existing full members downgrading to this cheaper option), potentially leading to a short-term drop in guaranteed annual revenue.

Strategic Recommendation

National Trust Connect: The Super-App Solution

National Trust Connect was selected as the preferred digital solution following comprehensive evaluation of all four proposed initiatives, as it uniquely addresses both critical strategic challenges facing the organization: declining youth engagement and over-reliance on physical visitation. This "phys-gital" super-app creates a unified ecosystem that seamlessly integrates on-site AR experiences with remote digital content, solving the dual crisis through a single, coherent platform rather than fragmented point solutions.

From a strategic perspective, the super-app architecture delivers unprecedented synergy between physical and digital engagement. The on-site AR Wildlife Quest component directly tackles the youth engagement crisis, transforming passive heritage observation into active, gamified exploration that resonates with Gen Z's preference for interactive experiences. Simultaneously, the National Trust Stream component monetizes global audiences who cannot visit physically—international supporters, mobility-impaired users, and time-poor professionals—creating a scalable revenue stream independent of site capacity constraints or weather conditions.

What distinguishes National Trust Connect from standalone solutions is its virtuous cycle design: physical visits earn points that unlock exclusive digital content (documentaries, behind-the-scenes footage), while digital engagement drives motivation for physical visitation to complete challenges and collect virtual artifacts. This bidirectional value flow creates stickiness that neither AR-only nor streaming-only solutions can achieve. Research validates this "hybrid paradox"—Gen Z simultaneously demands active, gamified physical experiences while consuming high volumes of digital wellness content at home. The super-app captures both behaviors within a unified brand experience.

From a business viability standpoint, the unified architecture enables significant operational efficiencies. A single app platform reduces development and maintenance costs compared to multiple discrete systems, while the shared user profile generates comprehensive behavioral data across both physical and digital touchpoints. This 360-degree view of member preferences—what sites they visit, what content they stream, what challenges they complete—enables unprecedented personalization and targeted engagement that strengthens retention and conversion metrics across the entire membership funnel.

Critically, National Trust Connect positions the organization for long-term competitive advantage. While rivals like English Heritage and Historic Royal Palaces invest in either physical or digital improvements, the super-app leapfrogs this binary choice by creating an integrated ecosystem that competitors will struggle to replicate. The platform's ability to deliver immediate customer value (gamified exploration), generate recurring revenue (streaming subscriptions), and collect rich behavioral data (personalization engine) establishes the National Trust not merely as a heritage site operator, but as a comprehensive cultural lifestyle brand serving audiences across the physical-digital spectrum.

Phase 2 – Linking to Phase 3

Phase 2 translates diagnosis into structured opportunity design. Building on the strategic tensions identified in Phase 1, alternative digital initiatives are generated and refined. Each concept is assessed using explicit evaluation criteria including feasibility, scalability, financial sustainability, user value, and alignment with mission objectives. The digital ideas evaluation tool formalises this comparison and makes trade offs transparent rather than intuitive. By narrowing multiple concepts into a single prioritised direction, this phase ensures that selection is logically justified and strategically coherent. The outcome is not simply a preferred idea but a defensible strategic choice supported by evidence. This validated direction then becomes the foundation for the detailed operationalisation and experience design developed in Phase 3.
