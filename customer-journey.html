Phase 3.1 ‚Äî Proposed Digital Solution

Integrated National Trust Stream Platform

We propose the development of National Trust Stream, an integrated digital membership platform that combines immersive virtual access with interactive on-site engagement. The platform functions as a hybrid ecosystem that extends the National Trust experience beyond physical boundaries while simultaneously enhancing engagement within its parks and heritage properties. Rather than positioning digital and physical experiences as substitutes, the initiative strategically integrates both into one unified membership structure.

National Trust Stream is delivered through a subscription-based mobile application. Members gain access to 360-degree virtual tours of parks and historic properties, allowing users to explore landscapes and heritage sites remotely through immersive, high-resolution environments. In addition, the platform offers live and on-demand "Slow Nature" streams, enabling users to experience seasonal changes, wildlife activity, and natural soundscapes in real time.

Key Differentiator

Augmented Reality Wildlife Discovery

A key differentiating feature of the platform is the integration of an augmented reality wildlife discovery experience for visitors physically present in parks. Using the same membership app, users can activate an interactive feature that allows them to discover virtual representations of native wildlife within the landscape. Completing challenges and identifying species unlocks rewards within the membership ecosystem, strengthening engagement and incentivising repeat visits. This gamified component directly addresses the growing expectation among younger, digitally native audiences for interactive and participatory experiences.

Strategic Rationale

Diversifying Revenue & Expanding Reach

Strategically, National Trust Stream responds to several structural challenges facing the organisation. Currently, revenue generation and engagement are heavily dependent on physical visitation. This creates vulnerability to weather conditions, seasonal fluctuations, and capacity constraints. By introducing a scalable digital membership model, the Trust diversifies its income streams and reduces reliance on footfall alone. The subscription-based structure generates recurring revenue while expanding the organisation's reach beyond geographic boundaries. International audiences, individuals with limited mobility, and time-constrained users can now meaningfully engage with National Trust content without the friction of travel.

The initiative also strengthens the Trust's competitive positioning within a broader digital leisure and subscription economy. Consumers increasingly expect on-demand, flexible access to content across devices. By offering immersive virtual experiences combined with educational and wellbeing services, the Trust enters the digital experience space while remaining aligned with its core mission of conservation, access, and public education.

AI-Powered Personalisation

Intelligent Recommendations & Tailored Experiences

An important dimension of National Trust Stream is its AI-powered personalisation capability. The platform incorporates machine learning algorithms that analyse user interaction data, such as viewing behaviour, session duration, and content preferences. Based on this data, the system recommends personalised virtual tours, educational sessions, and future site visits. During physical visits, the app can suggest tailored wildlife challenges or highlight areas aligned with a user's interests. This enhances perceived relevance and strengthens long-term engagement.

To enable these AI capabilities, the platform requires user interaction data collected within the app environment, including content consumption patterns, voluntary location data for AR features, and membership profile information. All data collection would operate under strict GDPR compliance, with transparent opt-in mechanisms and clear communication regarding data usage. Ethical concerns such as privacy risks and algorithmic bias would be mitigated through anonymisation protocols, data minimisation strategies, and regular auditing of recommendation systems. Ensuring transparency and trust is essential to maintaining the organisation's reputation and public credibility.

Value Creation

Multi-Layered Benefits for Users & the Organisation

From a customer perspective, National Trust Stream creates multi-layered value. Users gain continuous access to nature, heritage, and educational resources regardless of physical location. The platform supports lifelong learning, promotes mental wellbeing, and provides interactive exploration opportunities. Institutional accounts further extend this value proposition, enabling schools and universities to conduct virtual field trips and structured learning experiences aligned with curriculum objectives. This strengthens the Trust's educational impact while opening additional revenue opportunities.

From an organisational standpoint, the initiative is viable because it leverages existing assets. The National Trust already possesses extensive visual archives, expert knowledge, and physical landscapes that can be digitised and repurposed into scalable content. Rather than requiring entirely new infrastructure, the platform builds upon current resources and enhances their monetisation potential.

Overall, National Trust Stream represents a future-oriented digital transformation that balances accessibility, engagement, and financial resilience. By merging immersive digital access with interactive on-site participation, the solution transforms the National Trust into a hybrid conservation and digital experience ecosystem. It expands reach, deepens engagement, diversifies revenue, and reinforces the organisation's mission in a digitally evolving cultural landscape.
